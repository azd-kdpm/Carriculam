﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿ Session 15: Fiber Optic Basics
(15-1)

Title Slide - PoE & PoE Switches

  • Welcome to Power over Ethernet (PoE) technology.
  • Learn how to send power and data over one network cable.
  • Understand PoE standards, power budgets, and switch ports.
(15-2)

What is PoE?

  • PoE delivers electrical power over standard Cat5e/Cat6 network wires.
  • Eliminates the need to install separate power outlets near cameras.
  • Simplifies installation and reduces cabling costs.
(15-3)

PoE Standards (802.3af/at/bt)

  • Standard PoE (802.3af) provides up to 15.4 Watts per port.
  • PoE+ (802.3at) provides up to 30 Watts for PTZ cameras.
  • Hi-PoE (802.3bt) provides up to 60W or 90W for heavy heater cameras.
(15-4)

Power Budget Calculations

  • Total PoE Budget is the maximum power a switch can supply.
  • Add up all camera wattages to make sure it is under the switch limit.
  • Running over power budget causes cameras to reboot randomly.
(15-5)

Active vs Passive PoE

  • Active PoE negotiates power safely before sending electricity.
  • Passive PoE sends constant power (e.g. 24V) without checking.
  • Plugging non-PoE laptops into passive PoE ports can damage them.
(15-6)

PoE Pinout Wiring Modes

  • Mode A sends power over data pins (1,2 and 3,6).
  • Mode B sends power over spare pins (4,5 and 7,8).
  • Gigabit PoE sends power and data over all 4 wire pairs together.
(15-7)

PoE Splitters & Extenders

  • PoE Splitter separates 48V PoE into 12V DC power and Ethernet.
  • Use splitters to power non-PoE cameras from a PoE switch.
  • PoE Extenders increase cable distance beyond 100 meters.
(15-8)

Distance Limits & Voltage Drop

  • Maximum standard Ethernet cable distance is 100 meters.
  • Long cable runs cause voltage drops that turn off cameras at night.
  • Use CCTV Extend mode to reach 250 meters at 10Mbps speed.
(15-9)

PoE Switch Port Diagnostics

  • Check LED status lights to verify power and data link status.
  • Green light means data link; Amber light means PoE power active.
  • Flashing amber indicates power overload or cable short circuit.
(15-10)

Practical Lab Guide

  • Connect 4 IP cameras to a PoE switch.
  • Measure total power draw on the switch web management interface.
  • Test CCTV Extend Mode on a 200-meter Cat6 cable drum.
