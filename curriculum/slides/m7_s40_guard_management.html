Offline RFID Patrol Wands vs Real-Time 4G/GPS Solos

  • Rugged RFID Patrol Wands: Stainless steel/rubberized drop-proof wands (IP67) that log checkpoint taps offline.
  • Real-Time 4G/GPS Guard Phones: Live mobile apps transmitting GPS location, panic alerts, and photo logs instantly.
  • Deployment Selection: Use rugged offline wands for remote industrial sites; specify 4G GPS apps for high-security commercial towers.
Hardware Selection: Rugged IP67 wands withstand 2-meter drops onto concrete in harsh industrial facilities.
(40-3)

Checkpoint Tag Technologies: RFID, BLE & QR

  • 125kHz / 13.56MHz RFID Coin Tags: Weatherproof plastic coin tags mounted at perimeter gates, stairwells, and pump rooms.
  • BLE Proximity Beacons: Bluetooth beacons detecting guard presence automatically without manual tapping.
  • Encrypted Geofenced QR Codes: Anti-spoofing QR codes linked with smartphone GPS coordinates to prevent remote fake scans.
Installation Tip: Mount RFID checkpoint tags inside protective plastic wall housings at a height of 1.2 meters.
(40-4)

Guard Route Schedules & Time-Window Tolerances

  • Sequential vs Random Routes: Configures mandatory guard visit sequences (Checkpoint 1 → 2 → 3) or randomized patrols.
  • Time-Window Validation: Sets acceptable arrival windows (e.g. Checkpoint 4 must be visited between 02:15 and 02:30 AM).
  • Missed Checkpoint Alarm: Software fires immediate supervisor alert if guard fails to scan checkpoint within scheduled window.
Patrol Compliance: Time-window tolerances detect sleeping guards or delayed patrol routes automatically.

Man-Down Detection & SOS Panic Alerts

  • 3-Axis Accelerometer Sensor: Detects sudden falls or tilt angles exceeding 60° for over 30 seconds.
  • Automated Man-Down Alarm: Sounds pre-alarm chime before dispatching emergency GPS coordinates to control room.
  • Physical Panic Button: Dedicated red panic button sends instant distress alert during security confrontations.
Solo Guard Safety: 3-axis accelerometer sensors detect incapacitated or fallen guards automatically.

Troubleshooting Wand Upload & Battery Faults

  • Wand Unrecognized on USB Dock: Clean oxidized Pogo-pin contacts on wand base with isopropyl alcohol.
  • Unread Checkpoint Tags: Damaged 125kHz RFID coin tags mounted on steel posts; install non-metallic plastic spacers.
  • Memory Full Error: Perform monthly data purge after backing up database to prevent wand memory overflow.
Field Fix: ALWAYS install plastic spacers under RFID tags when mounting directly onto metal fences.
