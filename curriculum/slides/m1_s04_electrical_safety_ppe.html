Stage 1: Electrical Fundamentals & Wiring

(4-2)

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) Standards

  • Insulated Gloves: Class 0 (1000V rated) rubber gloves protect technicians during live terminal testing.
  • Safety Eyewear: Impact-resistant poly-carbonate glasses protect eyes against flash arcs and wire debris.
  • Insulated Footwear: Non-conductive EH rated safety boots prevent ground leakage paths through feet.
Safety Standard: Inspect insulated gloves for pinhole air leaks before entering live electrical rooms.
(4-3)

Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) Procedures & Padlocks

  • Isolate Power Source: Switch off breaker and physically apply a red LOTO padlock to prevent re-energization.
  • Danger Warning Tag: Attach a clear photo tag with technician name, contact, and work authorization date.
  • Verify Zero Energy: Test voltage with a meter to confirm complete de-energization before touching wires.
LOTO Rule: One lock per technician; never remove another technician's safety padlock.
(4-4)

Electric Shock Physiology & Body Resistance

  • Shock Thresholds: Current above 10mA causes muscle contraction (cannot let go); 50mA causes heart fibrillation.
  • Skin Resistance: Dry skin provides high resistance (100,000Î©); wet skin drops resistance to under 1,000Î©.
  • Current Path: Current passing hand-to-hand across the chest is most dangerous to heart rhythm.
Warning: Wet hands reduce skin resistance by 90%, drastically increasing shock hazard.
(4-5)

Electric Shock First Aid & Emergency Response

  • Disconnect Power Instantly: Shut off main breaker or use a non-conductive wooden hook to push victim away.
  • Never Touch Directly: Do NOT touch a live shock victim with bare hands while they remain in contact with power lines.
  • Emergency Call & CPR: Call emergency medical services immediately and begin CPR if victim is non-responsive.
Emergency First Step: Shut off main breaker before approaching an electrical shock casualty.
(4-6)

Panel Work Safety & Arc Flash Hazards

  • Arc Flash Explosions: High-current short circuits create explosive plasma discharges exceeding 10,000Â°C.
  • One-Hand Rule: Keep one hand in your pocket when probing live panels to avoid cross-chest current paths.
  • Panel Clearance: Maintain minimum 1 meter clean working clearance in front of all distribution panels.
Panel Rule: Keep 1 meter clear space in front of all electrical DB panels at all times.
(4-7)

Portable Electrical Tools & Insulation Class

  • Class II Double Insulated: Power tools with double square symbol require no earth ground wire.
  • Cable Inspection: Inspect power cords for cuts, exposed copper, or taped splices before use.
  • RCD Extension Reels: Plug portable drills and cutters into RCD protected power extension reels.
Tool Rule: Inspect power tool cables for damage before starting drilling or cutting tasks.
(4-8)

Hazardous Environments (Wet, Dusty, Explosive)

  • IP Waterproof Ratings: Use IP66 / IP67 enclosures for cameras and junction boxes exposed to rain or washdown.
  • ATEX Explosion-Proof: Use heavy cast flameproof enclosures in chemical plants and fuel storage yards.
  • Extra-Low Voltage (SELV): Use 12V / 24V SELV supplies in wet locations to eliminate shock hazard.
Environmental Rule: Use IP66 weatherproof enclosures for all outdoor junction boxes.
(4-9)

Safety Signage, Color Codes & Barrier Tape

  • Safety Color Yellow/Black: Indicates caution, physical hazard trip zones, and low headroom.
  • Safety Color Red/White: Indicates danger, fire equipment, and prohibited access zones.
  • Work Area Barricade: Erect temporary tape barricades around open trenching and live cable work zones.
Site Standard: Erect barricade tape around floor openings and open DB panel work areas.
(4-10)

Practical Hands-On Safety & LOTO Lab

  • LOTO Practice: Apply padlock and safety tag on a demo circuit breaker board.
  • Zero-Voltage Verification: Conduct 3-point live-dead-live meter test on test terminals.
  • PPE Inspection: Perform glove inflation leakage check and eye shield fitting.
Lab Goal: Achieve 100% score on LOTO isolation procedure and zero-voltage verification.
