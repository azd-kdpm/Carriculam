Cable Type Selection (Cat6, RG59, Armored)

  • Cat6 UTP / FTP: 4-pair twisted copper cable for IP cameras, supporting 1Gbps up to 100 meters.
  • Armored Cable (SWA): Steel Wire Armored cable for direct outdoor underground burial.
  • LSZH Jacket: Low Smoke Zero Halogen insulation prevents toxic gas emission during building fires.
Specification: Use LSZH jacket cables inside commercial buildings and airport terminals.
(7-3)

Conductor Gauge (AWG) & Copper vs CCA Wires

  • Pure Bare Copper (BC): 100% solid copper conductors deliver minimum DC resistance and full PoE power compliance.
  • Copper Clad Aluminum (CCA): Cheap aluminum wire coated with thin copper; high resistance causes PoE failure.
  • 23 AWG vs 24 AWG: 23 AWG Cat6 has thicker conductors, delivering lower DC resistance for PoE+ devices.
Warning: NEVER use CCA (Copper Clad Aluminum) cable for PoE CCTV installations.

(7-5)

Fire Safety Jackets (PVC vs LSZH vs Plenum)

  • PVC Jacket: Standard indoor insulation; releases dense black smoke and toxic hydrogen chloride gas during fires.
  • LSZH Jacket: Flame retardant Low Smoke Zero Halogen insulation for public buildings and transit tunnels.
  • Plenum (CMP): Special Teflon jacket for air plenum spaces above dropped ceilings.
Fire Code: Use CMP Plenum cables inside air-handling ceiling plenum spaces.
(7-6)

Conduit Fill Capacity (40% Rule) & Bending Radius

  • Conduit Fill Capacity: Do NOT exceed 40% conduit cross-section fill capacity for easy pulling.
  • Minimum Bending Radius: Maintain minimum 4x cable diameter bend radius for Cat6 (8x for fiber) to prevent internal kinks.
  • Pull Box Spacing: Install pull boxes every 30 meters or after maximum two 90-degree conduit bends.

Cable Tray Separation (Power vs Data)

  • 300mm Tray Gap: Maintain minimum 300mm distance between 230V AC power trays and Cat6 data trays.
  • 90-Degree Crossing: Cross power and data cables at a 90-degree right angle if intersection is unavoidable.
  • Metallic Tray Grounding: Earth ground all metal cable trays continuously along their entire length.
Noise Prevention Rule: Cross power and data cables at 90Â° right angles to minimize EMI induction.
(7-8)

Pulling Tension, Stress & Cable Accessories

  • Maximum Pulling Tension: Maximum 110 N (25 lbf) pulling tension on Cat6 copper cables to avoid stretching pairs.
  • Kinking & Untwisting: Do NOT untwist Cat6 pairs more than 13mm (0.5 inch) when terminating RJ45 plugs.
  • Cable Ties & Velcro: Use soft Velcro straps instead of tight nylon zip ties to prevent crushing cable jackets.
Termination Rule: Do not untwist Cat6 copper pairs more than 13mm at termination jacks.
(7-9)

Machine-Printed Heat-Shrink Cable Labeling

  • Machine-Printed Labels: Use thermal transfer printers for smear-proof, waterproof heat-shrink label sleeves.
  • Both-End Labeling: Label every cable within 100mm of both origin rack port and field device connector.
  • ANSI/TIA-606-B Standard: Follow standardized labeling syntax (e.g. 01-A-R02-P04).
Labeling Standard: Never use handwritten tape; use machine-printed heat-shrink sleeves.
