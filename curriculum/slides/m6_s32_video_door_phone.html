2-Wire HD VDP vs IP Network VDP

  • 2-Wire Hybrid System: Transmits power, audio, and HD video over existing 2-core doorbell wires without re-cabling.
  • IP Network VDP: Connects via standard Cat6 PoE cables, offering 1080p video, multi-tenant intercom, and mobile app integration.
  • Retrofit vs New Build: Use 2-wire VDP for old residential villa retrofits; specify IP VDP for new high-rise apartments.
Design Rule: 2-Wire VDP is ideal for upgrading old doorbell cables without tearing down plaster walls.

(32-5)

Electric Strike & Magnetic Lock Wiring

  • Dry Contact Relay Output: Door station incorporates NO/NC relay contacts to trigger electric door locks.
  • Fail-Secure vs Fail-Safe: Fail-secure electric strikes remain locked during power failure; fail-safe maglocks unlock for fire evacuation.
  • Flyback Protection Diode: Install 1N4007 flyback diode across lock coils to eliminate inductive voltage spike damage.
Wiring Rule: ALWAYS install a flyback diode across lock terminals to protect door station relay contacts.

Troubleshooting VDP Noise, Echo & Lock Faults

  • Audio Feedback Echo: Reduce microphone gain in door station web GUI to eliminate high-pitched acoustic feedback howling.
  • Video Grain & Voltage Drop: Thins 2-wire cables causing video flicker require installing a dedicated VDP power booster module.
  • Lock Failed to Release: Verify external 12V DC 2A power supply capability; door station internal power is insufficient for heavy maglocks.
Troubleshooting Tip: NEVER power heavy magnetic locks directly from VDP internal power terminals - use an external 12V PSU.
