Diagnosing "No Video" / Black Screen Conditions

  • Step 1 (Physical Power Check): Verify Ethernet port LED status lights and cover camera lens to test IR LED activation in dark.
  • Step 2 (Network Ping Test): Ping camera IP address to verify Layer 3 connectivity.
  • Step 3 (Camera Web GUI Direct Access): Type camera IP into browser to isolate whether issue is camera failure or NVR software fault.
Diagnostic Rule: If camera web GUI loads in browser but NVR shows Black Screen, the fault is ONVIF password or stream codec mismatch.
(29-3)

PoE Voltage Drop & Distance Limit Troubleshooting

  • Night Reboot Symptom: Camera works during day but reboots repeatedly at night when IR illuminators turn on (PoE power overload).
  • Resistance Voltage Drop: Long Cable runs (> 80 meters) using CCA (Copper Clad Aluminum) drop voltage below 44V DC minimum threshold.
  • PoE Tester Measurement: Measure real-time PoE voltage and wattage at camera RJ45 connector using Inline PoE Tester.
Hardware Fix: Replace CCA cable with 100% Solid Bare Copper CAT6 to eliminate night-time IR rebooting.
(29-4)

RTSP Stream Artifacts, Tearing & Frame Freezing

  • UDP Packet Loss Artifacts: Missing UDP video packets cause grey pixel smearing and horizontal tearing across screen.
  • Switch Buffer Congestion: Unmanaged switch buffering drops heavy I-Frames during complex motion scenes.
  • Switch to TCP Streaming: Configure RTSP protocol to use TCP transport (rtsp://... via TCP port 554) to guarantee packet delivery.
Configuration Fix: Change VMS stream transport protocol from UDP to TCP to eliminate video smearing.

Autofocus Drift, Lens Fogging & Moisture Condensation

  • Internal Condensation Fog: Moisture trapped inside glass dome turns into water droplets when internal heater fires.
  • Desiccant Pack Replacement: Replace silica gel desiccant packs inside camera housing during maintenance.
Maintenance Rule: ALWAYS replace silica gel desiccant packs inside dome cameras after opening housing cover outdoors.
(29-6)

IR Reflection Bleed & Ring Light Halo Faults

  • Rubber Foam Ring Misalignment: Rubber seal around camera lens MUST press firmly flat against plastic dome bubble.
  • IR Bleed Symptom: Infrared light reflects internally inside dome glass, producing bright white foggy circles on night video.
  • Nearby Obstruction Reflection: Soffits or wall edges within 0.5 meters reflect IR light back into camera lens.
Physical Fix: Ensure the rubber foam lens ring presses tight against the dome bubble to prevent IR bleed halos.
(29-7)

Image Sensor Noise, Snow & Ground Loop Grain

  • Digital Gain Noise (Snow): High AGC gain boost in extreme low light introduces static color speckles (snow).
  • Horizontal Rolling Hum Bars: AC ground potential differences produce 50Hz/60Hz rolling dark bars on analog/HD-TVI feeds.
  • Ground Loop Isolator: Install passive ground loop isolator on coax cable to eliminate AC ground hum interference.
Hardware Fix: Install a coax Ground Loop Isolator if horizontal dark bars roll vertically up the screen.
(29-8)

Network Cable Crosstalk & Incorrect Pair Pinouts

  • Split Pair Crimp Error: Crimp technician pins wire pairs straight-through without maintaining T568B twisted pair coupling.
  • Crosstalk Noise: Un-twisted pairs allow electromagnetic crosstalk, causing 100Mbps links to drop to 10Mbps or disconnect.
  • Wire Map Cable Tester: Test all 8 pins with wire map tester to verify pins 1,2 (Tx) and 3,6 (Rx) run on matched twisted pairs.
Wiring Rule: ALWAYS follow T568B color pinouts (Orange/White-Orange on Pins 1,2; Green/White-Green on Pins 3,6).
(29-9)

Firmware Corruption & Factory Reset Recovery

  • Bricked Camera Recovery: Power failure during firmware flash leaves camera in un-bootable bootloader loop.
  • Hardware Reset Button Hold: Press and hold physical camera reset button for 15 seconds while powering up to restore factory defaults.
  • TFTP Auto-Update Tool: Uses vendor TFTP recovery software (e.g. Hikvision TFTP) to re-flash corrupt firmware over Ethernet.
Recovery Tip: Use TFTP auto-update utility to recover bricked cameras that fail to boot after power outages.
