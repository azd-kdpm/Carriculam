LC Oscillator Physics & Metallic Vehicle Frequency Shift

  • Inductive Resonant Circuit (LC): Wire loop in roadway forms an inductor connected to detector's LC oscillator circuit (20kHz - 130kHz).
  • Eddy Current Principle: Metallic vehicle body driving over loop decreases loop inductance (L) and increases resonant frequency (f).
  • Relay Activation: Frequency shift exceeding threshold triggers internal relay contact to signal vehicle presence.
Physics Fact: Metallic car chassis decreases loop inductance, causing a frequency rise that triggers the detector relay.
(47-3)

Roadway Saw-Cutting: Slot Dimensions & Wire Turns Math

  • 4mm Slot Cutting: Cut 4mm wide x 40mm deep rectangular slot in asphalt/concrete using diamond saw blade.
  • 45° Corner Chamfering: Chisel 45-degree angle cuts at loop corners to prevent sharp asphalt edges from damaging wire insulation.
  • Wire Turns Calculation: 3 turns for 2m x 1m loop (80-150 μH target inductance); 4 turns for smaller loops < 2 meters.
Cutting Rule: ALWAYS chamfer 45° slot corners to prevent sharp asphalt edges from severing loop wire insulation.
(47-4)

Lead-In Cable Twisting & Cold-Pour Loop Sealing

  • 20 Turns/Meter Cable Twisting: Twist feeder cable wires at least 20 turns per meter from slot edge to detector to prevent false triggers.
  • XLPE / Silicone Insulated Wire: Use double-insulated 1.5mm² stranded copper wire rated for underground moisture resistance.
  • Polyurethane Cold-Pour Sealant: Fill saw-cut slot with waterproof flexible cold-pour sealant to protect wire from water intrusion and traffic heavy loads.
Installation Rule: Twist lead-in feeder wires at 20 turns/meter to cancel stray electromagnetic interference.
(47-5)

Single-Channel vs Dual-Channel Detectors & Directional Logic

  • Single-Channel Detector: Manages 1 loop (Safety Loop for anti-crushing or Presence Loop for opening).
  • Dual-Channel Detector (AB Logic): Connects to 2 loops (Loop A & Loop B) to determine vehicle entry/exit direction and count vehicles.
  • Directional Pulse Output: Fires direction-specific pulse relay only when vehicle moves from Loop A to Loop B.
Logic Selection: Dual-channel detectors prevent false opening from vehicles reversing out of entrance lanes.
(47-6)

Anti-Tailgating Fast Closing & Presence Safety Functions

  • Safety Loop (Under Arm): Keeps barrier arm 100% open as long as vehicle remains over loop; prevents arm dropping on car.
  • Fast Auto-Close (Trailing Edge): Barrier arm closes immediately (in 0.1s) after tail-end of vehicle clears safety loop.
  • Anti-Tailgating Interlock: Prevents second un-authorized tailgating vehicle from slipping through on single card tap.
Security Standard: Trailing-edge loop auto-close prevents unauthorized tailgating vehicles from following authorized cars.
(47-7)

Sensitivity Dip-Switch Tuning & Frequency Selection

  • 4-Level Sensitivity Switches: Adjust sensitivity to detect high-clearance SUVs (High Sensitivity) or filter out bicycles.
  • Frequency Channel Selection (Low / Medium / High): Prevents crosstalk interference between adjacent parallel loop lanes.
  • Automatic Frequency Tuning: Detector automatically tunes to optimal resonant frequency upon power-up.
Crosstalk Fix: Change frequency channels on adjacent loop detectors to prevent cross-lane false triggers.

Radar & Laser Loop Replacements (Surface Mount)

  • 79GHz Microwave Radar Sensors: Surface-mounted on barrier housing; eliminates asphalt saw-cutting completely.
  • Pedestrian vs Vehicle Filtering: Distinguishes between walking humans and metal cars to prevent false gate triggers.
  • Zero Roadway Damage: Ideal for decorative paver driveways, post-tensioned slab decks, and bridge decks where saw-cutting is banned.
Modern Alternative: 79GHz microwave radars replace saw-cut loops on post-tensioned concrete slabs.

Troubleshooting Loop Crosstalk, Frequency Drift & Moisture Faults

  • Detector LED Flashing Rapidly: Loop inductance out of range (< 50 μH or > 350 μH); re-count wire turns.
  • False Triggers Rain / Moisture: Water entered saw-cut slot due to unsealed cracks; re-seal slot with cold-pour polyurethane.
  • Intermittent Vehicle Drops: Increase sensitivity level or re-twist lead-in feeder wires.
Field Troubleshooting: Rapidly flashing red detector LEDs mean loop wire turns or inductance is out of range.
