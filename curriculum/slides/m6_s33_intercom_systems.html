(33-2)

SIP (Session Initiation Protocol) Intercom Standards

  • Open Standard Communication: Standard RFC 3261 SIP protocol allows intercoms to talk to Cisco/Asterisk IP-PBX phone systems.
  • SIP Call Signaling (Port 5060): Handles call initiation, ringing, session establishment, and teardown signaling.
  • RTP Audio Streaming: Transmits crystal-clear G.711 / G.722 wideband voice audio packets directly between stations.
Industry Standard: Specify SIP-compliant intercoms to allow seamless integration into corporate IP phone networks.

  • Multi-Call Queue Management: Manages simultaneous incoming visitor calls with priority queueing and call holding.
  • Broadcast Paging: Allows security guards to broadcast emergency voice announcements to all indoor apartment monitors.
Control Desk Standard: Guard master consoles feature gooseneck mics and live camera previews before answering calls.

Multi-Tenant IP PBX & Virtual Extensions

  • Embedded / Cloud IP-PBX: Assigns unique 4-digit extension numbers (e.g. Ext 1001 to 1500) to each building apartment.
  • Group Calling & Parallel Ring: Visitor call rings indoor monitor, tenant mobile phone, and wall tablet simultaneously.
  • Voicemail & Call Forwarding: Leaves audio message for tenants if call goes unanswered after 30 seconds.
PBX Feature: Parallel ringing ensures tenants never miss a delivery driver call whether at home or away.
(33-6)

Acoustic Noise Suppression & Full-Duplex Audio

  • AEC (Acoustic Echo Cancellation): Prevents speaker audio from leaking back into microphone, eliminating echo.
  • Full-Duplex Speech: Enables both parties to speak and hear simultaneously without voice clipping.
  • Wideband G.722 HD Voice: Delivers 7kHz HD voice clarity for superior speech intelligibility in noisy environments.
Audio Quality: Full-duplex AEC algorithms allow natural conversation without audio cutting out.

  • Pop-Up Video on Call: VMS automatically pops up intercom camera feed on guard screen the moment SOS button is pressed.

(33-8)

Redundant Network Trunking & Backup Power

  • Dual Ethernet Ports: Industrial intercoms feature dual RJ45 ports for RSTP ring network redundancy.
  • PoE Pass-Through: Powers secondary devices (e.g. card reader or auxiliary camera) from single PoE drop.
  • Centralized UPS Backing: Core intercom switches powered by online double-conversion UPS to maintain communications during blackouts.
Reliability Rule: Intercom systems MUST remain operational during building power outages for emergency communications.
(33-9)

SIP Registration & NAT Traversal Troubleshooting

  • SIP 403 Forbidden: Invalid extension password or mismatched SIP domain settings in intercom web GUI.
  • One-Way Audio Fault: NAT router blocking incoming RTP audio ports; enable STUN server or SIP ALG bypass.
  • Codec Mismatch (No Audio): Ensure both intercom and IP-PBX support identical audio codecs (G.711u / G.711a).
Troubleshooting Tip: One-way audio in SIP intercoms is almost always caused by NAT router port blocking.
