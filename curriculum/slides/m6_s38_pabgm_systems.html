8-Ohm Low Impedance vs 100V Constant Voltage Line

  • 8-Ohm Low Impedance (Hi-Fi): Used for home theater; high current causes severe power loss over cable runs > 30 meters.
  • 100V Constant Voltage Line: High voltage / low current distribution allows connecting 50+ speakers over 1,000+ meters.
  • Parallel Speaker Wiring: All 100V line speakers wire in simple parallel across single speaker cable run.
Audio Rule: ALWAYS specify 100V constant voltage line systems for commercial building PA/BGM installations.
(38-3)

Speaker Step-Down Transformers & Power Taps

  • Line Matching Transformer: Every 100V speaker includes a step-down transformer converting 100V line to 8-ohm cone voltage.
  • Selectable Power Taps: Multi-tap transformer wires (e.g. 6W, 3W, 1.5W) adjust individual speaker volume levels.
  • Acoustic SPL Calculation: Tap ceiling speakers to 3W in quiet corridors; tap to 6W in noisy cafeterias.
Tapping Rule: Adjust transformer power taps (6W/3W/1.5W) to achieve even sound pressure level (SPL) across zones.
(38-4)

Amplifier Sizing & 20% Power Headroom Math

  • Total Speaker Wattage Sum: Add up wattage taps of all connected 100V line speakers (e.g. 40 speakers x 3W = 120W).
  • 20% Overhead Headroom: Multiply total speaker load by 1.25 to determine required minimum amplifier power rating.
  • Amplifier Sizing Formula: 120 Watts Speaker Load x 1.25 = Minimum 150 Watt 100V Line Power Amplifier.
Calculation Formula: Total Speaker Taps x 1.25 = Minimum Amplifier Power (Prevents amplifier clipping and thermal shutdown).
(38-5)

Multi-Zone Paging Selectors & Matrix Routers

  • Zone Routing Matrix: Divides building into independent audio zones (Zone 1: Lobby, Zone 2: Offices, Zone 3: Parking).
  • Selective Desk Paging Microphones: Paging console buttons allow security guards to announce to Zone 1 or All-Call.
  • BGM Override: Voice paging microphone automatically mutes background music while announcement is active.
Paging Logic: Microphone priority circuits automatically mute background music during live voice announcements.
(38-6)

EN 54-16 Voice Evacuation (VA) & Fire Interface

  • EN 54-16 Compliance: European standard for automated emergency voice evacuation systems in commercial buildings.
  • Fire Alarm Trigger Interface: Fire alarm panel contact triggers pre-recorded phased evacuation voice messages.
  • Fire-Rated Cabling & Line Monitoring: Uses red FP200 fire-resistant cable with continuous 20kHz pilot-tone line monitoring.
Life Safety: EN 54-16 Voice Evacuation broadcasts clear spoken evacuation instructions instead of confusing bells.
(38-7)

Speaker Selection: Ceiling, Wall Box, Horn & Sound Projectors

  • Flush Ceiling Coaxial Speakers: Aesthetic wide-dispersion speakers for office acoustic tile ceilings.
  • Re-entrant Paging Horns: High-efficiency weather-proof horns (110dB SPL) for noisy outdoor parking lots and factories.
  • Bidirectional Sound Projectors: Dual-cone narrow beam speakers designed for long corridors and train platforms.
Environment Selection: Use re-entrant horns for outdoor parking lots; use flush ceiling speakers for indoor offices.
(38-8)

IP Network Audio Transmitters & Dante Audio Protocol

  • Dante Digital Audio Networking: Transmits uncompressed multi-channel low-latency digital audio over standard Cat6 IP networks.
  • Native IP PoE Speakers: Individual IP ceiling speakers with built-in PoE amplifiers, SIP decoding, and web GUIs.
  • Decentralized Architecture: Eliminates heavy copper 100V home-run speaker cables in large multi-building campuses.
Future Tech: Dante IP audio allows routing 64 digital audio channels over a single Ethernet cable.
(38-9)

Troubleshooting 100V Line Shorts, Distortion & Hum

  • Amplifier Thermal Shutdown: Caused by 100V speaker line short circuit or exceeding amplifier maximum wattage rating.
  • Audio Distortion & Clipping: Speaker tapped directly to 8-ohm output instead of 100V transformer tap terminals.
  • Impedance Meter Measurement: Measure total speaker line impedance in ohms using 1kHz AC Audio Impedance Meter (NOT standard DMM).
Measurement Rule: Measure 100V speaker line load using a 1kHz AC Audio Impedance Meter to detect hidden line shorts.
