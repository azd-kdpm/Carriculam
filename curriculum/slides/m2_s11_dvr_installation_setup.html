Coaxial Cable Anatomy (RG59 vs RG6)

  • 95% Solid Copper Braid: High density bare copper braid shields video signal against EMI noise.
  • Solid Bare Copper Core: 20 AWG solid copper center conductor delivers 75-Ohm characteristic impedance.
  • Shotgun Coax (RG59 + 2C): Combines RG59 video coax with 18 AWG 2-core power cable in one zip jacket.
Specification: Use RG59 Shotgun coax (coax + 2-core power) for clean single-cable runs.

Twist-On BNC Hazards & Signal Failure

  • High Failure Rate: Screw threads cut coaxial copper braid strands, creating intermittent open circuits.
  • Impedance Mismatch: Loose physical contact causes 75-Ohm impedance jumps, resulting in double-image video ghosting.
  • Professional Ban: Twist-on BNC connectors are banned in commercial security specifications.
Warning: NEVER use twist-on BNC connectors in professional security camera installations.
(11-6)

DC Power Pigtails & Screw Terminal Plugs

  • 2.1mm x 5.5mm Standard: Standard DC barrel jack dimensions for 12V security cameras.
  • Center Positive (+): Center pin carries +12V DC positive; outer sleeve connects to 0V ground (-).
  • Screw Terminal Adapters: Male/Female DC pigtails with screw terminals speed up 2-core power wiring.
Polarity Rule: Double check DC pigtail polarity (+ Red / - Black) before switching on PSU.

Passive Video Baluns over Cat6 UTP

  • Impedance Matching Transformer: Converts 75-Ohm unbalanced coax to 100-Ohm balanced Cat6 twisted pair.
  • 4 Cameras on 1 Cat6: Transmit 4 separate analog HD video channels over one 4-pair Cat6 cable.
  • Distance Capability: Supports up to 200m HD-TVI / HD-CVI / AHD video without external power.
Cabling Efficiency: Run 4 analog cameras over a single 4-pair Cat6 cable using video baluns.
(11-8)

Ground Loop Hum Bars & Isolator Hardware

  • Ground Potential Difference: Voltage offset between camera ground and DVR ground causes rolling black lines.
  • Rolling Hum Bars: Black horizontal bars rolling slowly up or down the video screen.
  • Ground Loop Isolator: Transformer-based isolator breaks ground loops, instantly clearing rolling hum bars.
Troubleshooting: Install inline BNC Ground Loop Isolators to eliminate rolling black hum bars.
(11-9)

Cable Labeling & BNC Strain Relief Boots

  • Strain Relief Boots: Rubber boots cover BNC crimp ferrules to prevent wire flexing stress and moisture ingress.
  • Heat-Shrink Labeling: Machine-printed heat-shrink tags placed 50mm behind BNC connectors.
  • Color-Coded Boots: Use distinct boot colors (Red/Blue/Yellow) to identify camera channels instantly.
Best Practice: Always slide BNC strain relief boots onto cable before stripping and crimping.
