Digital Multimeter (DMM) Layout & Controls

  • Display Screen: 3.5-digit LCD screen showing numerical readings, units (V, A, Î©), and low battery icons.
  • Rotary Function Dial: Selects test modes (DC Voltage, AC Voltage, DC Amperes, Resistance, Continuity).
  • Probe Jacks: Black probe goes to COM (Common ground); Red probe connects to V/Î©/mA or 10A High Current jack.
Safety Rule: Always plug the black lead into the COM jack before connecting test leads.
(3-3)

Measuring DC & AC Voltage

  • Parallel Connection: Place multimeter test probes across (in parallel with) the component or power terminals.
  • DC Voltage Mode (V=): Used for testing 12V DC power supplies, batteries, and camera power inputs.
  • AC Voltage Mode (V~): Used for testing 230V AC mains wall outlets and transformer secondary outputs.
Meter Rule: Always measure voltage with meter connected in parallel across live terminals.
(3-4)

Measuring Current (Series Connection)

  • Series Break Connection: Break the electrical circuit and place the multimeter in series so current passes through the meter.
  • 10A High Current Jack: Move red lead to the 10A jack when measuring currents above 200mA to avoid blowing internal fuses.
  • Internal Fuse Protection: DMMs use fast-acting ceramic fuses (e.g. 500mA / 10A) to protect internal meter shunts.
Warning: Connecting a current-mode multimeter in parallel across power supply terminals WILL blow the internal fuse!
(3-5)

Resistance & Continuity Testing

  • Power OFF Rule: ALWAYS turn off circuit power before measuring resistance or testing wire continuity.
  • Continuity Beeper Mode: Meter emits an audible beep sound if wire resistance is less than 30 Ohms (< 30Î©).
  • Cable Break Detection: Use continuity mode to locate broken conductors or short circuits in long cable runs.
Safety Rule: Never measure resistance on a live powered circuit to prevent damaging the meter.
(3-6)

Diode & Semiconductor Testing

  • Forward Bias Test: Red probe on Anode (+), Black probe on Cathode (-); meter displays forward voltage drop (â‰ˆ 0.6V for silicon).
  • Reverse Bias Test: Reverse the probes; meter displays 'OL' (Open Loop), confirming the diode blocks current.
  • LED Testing: Testing Light Emitting Diodes in forward bias causes the LED to glow faintly.
Diagnostic Tip: A shorted diode reads 0.00V in both directions; an open diode reads 'OL' in both directions.
(3-7)

Cable Tracing & Short Circuit Fault Finding

  • Short Circuit Fault: Unintended direct contact between Phase and Neutral or Positive and Negative wires.
  • Pair Identification: Twist two wire ends together at one end of a cable and test continuity at the far end.
  • Shield Ground Integrity: Test continuity between cable foil shield and Earth busbar.
Technician Hack: Short the far end wire pair to quickly identify multi-core cable strands using continuity beep.
(3-8)

Battery Load Testing & Health Checks

  • Open Circuit Voltage: A fully charged 12V SLA battery reads 13.5V - 13.8V on float charge.
  • Voltage Drop Under Load: Battery voltage dropping below 10.5V under load indicates a dead battery cell.
  • Internal Resistance Check: Higher internal resistance causes heavy voltage drops during power failure switchover.
Maintenance Rule: Replace 12V backup batteries if voltage drops below 11V during load testing.
(3-9)

Multimeter Safety Ratings (CAT I to CAT IV)

  • CAT Category Rating: Defines the high-voltage transient withstand capability of the multimeter.
  • CAT II / CAT III Standard: Use CAT III 600V or CAT III 1000V rated meters for building electrical distribution panels.
  • Test Lead Quality: Use double-insulated silicone test leads with finger guards and shrouded banana plugs.
Safety Standard: Always use minimum CAT III 600V rated multimeters when working inside main electrical DBs.

Practical Hands-On Lab Exercises

  • Lab Task 2: Measure DC current draw of an active security camera in 10A series mode.
  • Lab Task 3: Locate hidden cable faults and test diode forward voltage drops on a test board.
