(16-2)

Power over Ethernet (PoE) Principles

  • Combined Power & Data: Transmits 48V DC power and Ethernet data over a single Cat5e/Cat6 cable up to 100 meters.
  • Eliminates AC Outlets: Eliminates the need to install 230V AC power outlets at high camera mounting locations.
  • PSE vs PD: Switch is the Power Sourcing Equipment (PSE); Camera is the Powered Device (PD).
Key Term: PSE = Power Sourcing Equipment (Switch); PD = Powered Device (Camera).
(16-3)

IEEE PoE Standards (802.3af / at / bt)

  • 802.3af (Standard PoE): Delivers up to 15.4W at port (12.95W at camera); suitable for standard fixed dome cameras.
  • 802.3at (PoE+): Delivers up to 30W at port (25.5W at camera); required for IR bullet and varifocal cameras.
  • 802.3bt (Hi-PoE / Ultra PoE): Delivers 60W to 90W at port; required for outdoor PTZ speed domes with internal heaters.
Selection Guide: Standard Dome = 15.4W (af) | IR Bullet = 30W (at) | Outdoor PTZ = 60W-90W (bt).
(16-4)

PoE Wiring Modes (Mode A vs Mode B)

  • Mode A (Endspan): Transmits DC power over data pairs (Pins 1,2 and Pins 3,6) using phantom power.
  • Mode B (Midspan): Transmits DC power over spare unused pairs (Pins 4,5 positive and Pins 7,8 negative).
  • 4-Pair PoE (802.3bt): Delivers high current by transmitting DC power across ALL 4 pairs (1,2,3,6 + 4,5,7,8).
Wiring Rule: Always crimp Cat6 cables to T568B standard to ensure full PoE compatibility.
(16-5)

Switch Total PoE Power Budget Calculations

  • Total Power Budget: Maximum combined wattage a PoE switch can supply across all ports simultaneously (e.g. 250W).
  • Over-Subscription Danger: Connecting sixteen 25W PoE+ cameras (400W total) to a 250W switch causes port shutdowns.
  • Power Budget Math: Sum of all camera peak wattages (including night IR) must NOT exceed 80% of switch total PoE budget.
Sizing Formula: Total Connected Camera Wattage / 0.8 = Minimum Switch PoE Power Budget.
(16-6)

Extended Distance PoE (250m CCTV Mode)

  • Standard 100m Limit: IEEE Ethernet rules restrict Cat6 cable runs to a maximum of 100 meters (328 ft).
  • Extended / CCTV Mode Switch: Drops port speed from 100Mbps to 10Mbps, extending PoE distance to 250 meters.
  • 10Mbps Bandwidth Capacity: 10Mbps is plenty of bandwidth for a single 4MP/8MP IP camera stream (~4-6Mbps).
Distance Extension: Enable 'CCTV / Extended Mode' on switch ports for long cable runs up to 250 meters.
(16-7)

PoE Extenders & Midspan Injectors

  • Single-Port PoE Injector: Adds PoE power to a non-PoE network switch port for a single IP camera.
  • Inline PoE Extender: Powered by incoming PoE; daisy-chains Cat6 distance another 100 meters without AC power.
  • Outdoor Enclosures: Use IP67 waterproof rated PoE extenders for outdoor pole and perimeter runs.
Field Solution: Use inline PoE extenders to reach cameras up to 300m without requiring local 230V AC power.
(16-8)

Managed PoE Port Monitoring & Remote Reboot

  • Real-Time Wattage Monitoring: View exact real-time power consumption (Watts) per port on the switch web GUI.
  • PoE Watchdog (PD Alive): Switch automatically pings cameras; if a camera freezes, the switch power-cycles the port.
  • Remote Hard Reboot: Power cycle a frozen camera remotely from your office without sending a technician to site.
Technician Saver: Enabling 'PoE Watchdog' automatically reboots frozen cameras without site visits.
(16-9)

PoE Surge Protection & Lightning Grounding

  • Outdoor Surge Risk: Outdoor Cat6 cable runs act as antennas for lightning electrostatic surge induction.
  • Inline Gigabit PoE Surge Arrestors: Installed at both building entrance and camera end; grounded to Earth busbar.
  • Shielded FTP Cable: Use Shielded Foil Twisted Pair (FTP) Cat6 cable with metallic RJ45 drain wire plugs.
Protection Rule: Ground outdoor PoE surge protectors to main Earth busbar using 4sqmm ground wire.
