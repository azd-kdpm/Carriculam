(2-2)

Principles of Safety Earthing

  • Human Safety: Protects personnel from fatal electric shocks by diverting fault currents into the ground.
  • Equipment Protection: Stabilizes system voltage and prevents high-voltage spikes from damaging sensitive ELV boards.
  • Zero Voltage Reference: Provides a clean 0V potential ground plane for security cameras and control panels.
Core Principle: Fault current always follows the path of least electrical resistance to ground.
(2-3)

Chemical Bentonite Earth Pit Design

  • Copper Electrode Rod: Solid copper or copper-bonded steel rod driven vertically into the earth pit.
  • Bentonite Compound: Moisture-retaining chemical compound surrounding the rod to maintain low soil resistivity year-round.
  • Target Resistance: Earth pit resistance MUST be less than 5 Ohms (< 5Î©) for general systems and < 1Î© for sensitive data centers.
Standard: Maintain earth pit resistance below 5 Ohms for ELV control equipment.
(2-4)

Equipotential Bonding & Busbars

  • Equipotential Busbar (EEB): Solid copper busbar where all metallic enclosures, racks, and shields connect together.
  • Ground Loop Prevention: Eliminates dangerous voltage potential differences between different metal equipment racks.
  • Bonding Conductors: Heavy-gauge green/yellow copper earth cables linking racks directly to the main earthing terminal.
Best Practice: Connect all server and CCTV equipment racks to a central copper earthing busbar.
(2-5)

Surge Protection Devices (SPD)

  • Voltage Surge Source: Lightning strikes and grid switching cause massive high-voltage transients on power and data lines.
  • MOV & GDT Technology: Metal Oxide Varistors and Gas Discharge Tubes clamp excessive surge voltages within nanoseconds.
  • SPD Placement: Install Type 1/2 SPDs at main power DBs and dedicated Ethernet/Coax SPDs before outdoor cameras.
Rule: Install inline network SPDs on all outdoor IP cameras mounted on high metal poles.
(2-6)

Lightning Protection System (LPS)

  • Air Terminal Rods: Copper or aluminum lightning arrestors mounted at the highest roof elevation.
  • Down Conductors: Heavy copper tape routing lightning current directly down exterior building walls.
  • Separation Distance: Keep lightning down-conductors isolated from CCTV and network cable trays to prevent inductive arc-over.
Warning: Never run CCTV or security cables inside the same conduit as lightning down-conductors.
(2-7)

Earth Resistance Measurement (Megger Test)

  • 3-Pole Fall of Potential Method: Uses a Earth Resistance Tester (Megger) with Current (C) and Potential (P) auxiliary spikes.
  • Testing Spikes Spacing: Drive test spikes 5 meters and 10 meters away from the earth electrode pit under test.
  • Periodic Maintenance: Test earth pit resistance every 6 months to ensure low-resistance conductivity.
Procedure: Disconnect the earth electrode from the main busbar before performing the Megger resistance test.
(2-8)

Earth Leakage Protection (RCCB / ELCB)

  • Differential Current Sensing: Residual Current Circuit Breaker compares Live and Neutral current flow in real time.
  • Trip Threshold: 30mA RCCBs trip instantly (within 30ms) if current leaks to ground to save human lives.
  • False Tripping Prevention: Separate camera power circuits from heavy motor lines to avoid nuisance RCCB trips.
Safety Standard: Use 30mA RCCB breakers for all field-installed outdoor power outlets.
(2-9)

Ground Loop Noise in CCTV Systems

  • Ground Loop Cause: Occurs when cameras and recorders are grounded at two different ground potential points.
  • Symptom: Rolling hum bars, diagonal video lines, and distorted audio on camera channels.
  • Ground Loop Isolator: Install video ground loop isolators on coaxial lines to break unwanted ground current paths.
Troubleshooting Fix: Install a passive ground loop isolator to remove rolling video lines.
(2-10)

Practical Lab & Field Commissioning

  • Earth Resistance Testing: Conduct a live 3-spike Megger test on the lab earth pit.
  • SPD Installation: Wire a 230V AC SPD module inside a power distribution board.
  • Ground Loop Troubleshooting: Induce a ground loop on a test camera and eliminate it using an isolator module.
Lab Goal: Successfully measure pit resistance under 5 Ohms and verify RCCB trip times.
