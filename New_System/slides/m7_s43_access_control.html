(43-2)

Multi-Door IP Controller Board Architecture

  • 1-Door, 2-Door & 4-Door Boards: On-board ARM microprocessor processing local access decisions offline.
  • Dedicated Inputs / Outputs: Separate terminal blocks for Readers (Wiegand/RS485), Lock Relays, REX Buttons, and Door Sensors.
  • Non-Volatile Event Memory: Stores up to 100,000 user credentials and 200,000 offline event logs during network failure.
Architecture Fact: Access controllers make local authentication decisions in < 0.1 seconds using local RAM memory.
(43-3)

Legacy Wiegand 26/34 Protocol vs Modern OSDP v2

  • Legacy Wiegand 26/34 Bit: 1-way unencrypted pulse protocol over Data0/Data1 lines; vulnerable to wiretapping attacks.
  • OSDP v2 (Open Supervised Device Protocol): Bi-directional RS485 protocol with AES-128 Secure Channel encryption.
  • Supervised Line Monitoring: OSDP continuously polls reader health; immediately alerts if reader cable is cut or tampered.
Security Standard: Specify OSDP v2 with AES-128 encryption to eliminate Wiegand wiretapping vulnerabilities.
(43-4)

Electric Strike & Magnetic Lock Power & Relay Wiring

  • Fail-Secure (Electric Strike): Power applied to UNLOCK (NO contact); stays locked during power blackout for physical security.
  • Fail-Safe (Magnetic Lock): Power applied to LOCK (NC contact); unlocks automatically during power failure for fire escape.
  • 1N4007 Protection Diode / MOV: Suppresses inductive back-EMF voltage spikes when lock coil de-energizes.
Wiring Rule: ALWAYS install a 1N4007 diode (DC) or MOV (AC) across lock terminals to protect controller relays.
(43-5)

Request-to-Exit (REX) Buttons & Motion Sensors

  • Momentary REX Push Button: Installed inside room to break power to maglock or signal controller to release door.
  • Passive Infrared (PIR) REX Sensor: Mounted above door inside to automatically release lock when a person approaches.
  • Hardwired Emergency Break-Glass: Green emergency break-glass unit wired directly in series with maglock power line for mechanical bypass.
Safety Mandatory: Emergency green break-glass units MUST cut maglock power directly, independent of software.
(43-6)

Door Position Switches (DPS) & Anti-Tailgating Rules

  • Magnetic Door Contact (DPS): Detects whether door is physically Open or Closed using NC reed switch.
  • Door Forced Open (DFO) Alarm: Triggers audible alarm and camera recording if door is opened without valid card tap.
  • Door Held Open (DHO) Alarm: Sounds buzzer if door remains propped open longer than 30 seconds.
Security Feature: Magnetic door contacts generate instant alerts for forced or propped-open doors.
(43-7)

Anti-Passback (APB) Rules & Interlock Airlocks

  • Hard Anti-Passback: Prevents user from passing card back to a friend; requires valid IN tap before allowing another IN tap.
  • Soft Anti-Passback: Allows entry on duplicate card tap but logs violation warning on security guard console.
  • 2-Door Interlock (Mantrap / Airlock): Door B cannot be opened until Door A is completely closed and locked (used in banks/cleanrooms).
High Security: 2-door mantrap interlocks ensure outer street doors close fully before inner vault doors unlock.
(43-8)

Fire Alarm Panel Interface & Central Emergency Release

  • Dedicated FACP Fire Relay Input: Connects dry contact from Fire Alarm Panel directly to access control power supply.
  • Hardware Power Cutout: FACP trip instantly removes 12V DC power from ALL maglocks across the building.
  • NFPA 101 Life Safety Code: Guarantees free egress during fires even if access control software or CPU freezes.
Life Safety Code: FACP fire trip MUST physically drop 12V power to all maglocks at the power supply level.
(43-9)

Troubleshooting Wiegand Data0/Data1 Swaps & Voltage Drops

  • Reader Beeps but No Card Log: Data0 (Green) and Data1 (White) wires reversed at terminal block.
  • Reader Reset / Low LED Dimming: Thins 12V power wires causing voltage drop < 10.8V under load; install local 12V PSU.
  • Ground Loop Noise: Ensure reader ground (GND) and controller ground are tied together to common reference voltage.
Troubleshooting Fix: Swapping Data0 (Green) and Data1 (White) wires resolves 90% of "reader beeps but no card read" issues.
