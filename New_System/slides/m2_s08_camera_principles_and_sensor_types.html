Image Sensor Technologies (CCD vs CMOS)

  • CMOS Sensors: Low power consumption, integrated digital signal processing, and fast frame rates.
  • CCD Sensors: High light sensitivity and global shutter, but higher power draw and manufacturing cost.
  • Modern CCTV Standard: Advanced STARVIS CMOS sensors dominate current security camera designs.
Industry Standard: Modern CMOS sensors match CCD image quality while using 80% less power.
(8-3)

Sensor Size & Aspect Ratio Impact

  • Sensor Dimensions: Larger 1/1.8" sensors capture more photons than 1/2.8" sensors, improving night vision.
  • Pixel Size (Microns): Larger physical pixel sizes deliver higher signal-to-noise ratio in low light.
  • Aspect Ratios (16:9 vs 4:3): 16:9 widescreen format matches modern HD monitors without letterbox stretching.
Rule: Larger sensor sizes (e.g. 1/1.8") provide far superior low-light performance.

(8-5)

Lens Aperture (F-Stop) & Light Sensitivity

  • Aperture Rating (F-Number): Indicates iris opening size; smaller F-numbers pass more light to the sensor.
  • F1.0 Super Aperture: ColorVu cameras use F1.0 lenses to capture 4x more light than standard F2.0 lenses.
  • Depth of Field: Large apertures (F1.0) create shallow depth of field; small apertures (F8) keep entire scene in focus.
Optics Rule: F1.0 lenses let in 4 times more light than F2.0 lenses for full-color night vision.
(8-6)

Electronic Shutter Speed & Motion Blur

  • Shutter Duration: Controls exposure time per frame (e.g. 1/50s for general surveillance vs 1/1000s for traffic).
  • Fast Motion Capture: License plate cameras require 1/1000s shutter speed to freeze fast-moving vehicles.
  • Night Exposure Tradeoff: Slower shutter speeds brighten night images but introduce severe motion blur ghosting.
LPR Setting: Use 1/1000s shutter speed for capturing moving vehicle license plates without motion blur.
(8-7)

Wide Dynamic Range (True WDR vs DWDR)

  • High Contrast Scenes: Solves dark silhouetting when subjects stand in front of bright glass windows.
  • True Hardware WDR (120dB): Sensor captures double exposure frames (long + short exposure) and merges them.
  • Digital WDR (DWDR): Software gamma curve manipulation; less effective than true 120dB hardware WDR.
Specification Standard: Require minimum 120dB True WDR for entrance doors facing outdoor sunlight.
(8-8)

Infrared (IR) & Low-Light Night Vision

  • IR Cut Filter (ICR): Mechanically switches out daytime IR filter at night to receive 850nm IR LED light.
  • EXIR / Smart IR: Adjusts IR LED power dynamically to prevent over-exposing subjects close to the camera.
  • Full-Color Night Vision: Uses F1.0 aperture + warm supplemental white light to deliver 24/7 color video.
Feature Tip: Smart IR prevents facial washout when people walk close to night vision cameras.
(8-9)

Digital Noise Reduction (2D-DNR & 3D-DNR)

  • 2D-DNR (Spatial Filtering): Filters static noise within individual image frames; best for moving subjects.
  • 3D-DNR (Temporal Filtering): Compares consecutive frames to eliminate snow noise without reducing sharp detail.
  • Bandwidth & Storage Savings: 3D-DNR removes random pixel noise, reducing video compression stream bitrates by 30%.
Storage Advantage: 3D-DNR saves up to 30% storage space by eliminating night video noise.
