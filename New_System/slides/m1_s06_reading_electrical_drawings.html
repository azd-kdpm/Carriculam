Stage 1: Electrical Fundamentals & Wiring

(6-2)

Single-Line Diagram (SLD) Fundamentals

  • Simplified Overview: Uses standard electrical symbols to show complete power distribution paths.
  • Main Busbar & Breakers: Displays main incoming ACB/MCCBs down to sub-distribution DBs and camera power rails.
  • Rating Markings: Indicates cable sizes (e.g. 4C 16sqmm) and circuit breaker Amp ratings (e.g. 63A TP).
Key Concept: SLDs provide the master roadmap for building electrical distribution systems.
(6-3)

Standard ELV Symbols & Legend Tables

  • Camera Symbols: Dome, Bullet, and PTZ camera symbols on architectural CAD drawings.
  • Access Control Symbols: RFID reader, Electromagnetic lock, Request-to-Exit, and door contacts.
  • Drawing Legend: Always refer to the drawing legend block to verify exact symbol definitions.
Rule: Check project drawing legend table before interpreting CAD symbols.
(6-4)

Architectural Floor Plans & Device Placement

  • CAD Layering: Overlay CCTV and security device symbols onto architectural building layouts.
  • Field of View Cones: Verify camera FOV coverage and blind spots on floor plan drawings.
  • Mounting Heights: Check drawing elevation callouts for wall, ceiling, or pole mounting heights.
Best Practice: Verify camera mounting heights against interior ceiling architectural drawings.
(6-5)

Schematic Wiring Diagrams & Terminal Loops

  • Point-to-Point Schematics: Detail exact wire terminal connections between devices and panels.
  • Terminal Numbering: Matches physical terminal block numbers inside control enclosures (e.g. TB1-1, TB1-2).
  • Relay Logic Loops: Shows Normally Open (NO) and Normally Closed (NC) relay contacts.
Technician Rule: Wire panels strictly according to point-to-point schematic terminal numbers.
(6-6)

Cable Schedule Tables & Tagging Systems

  • Cable Tag Number: Unique identifier for every cable run (e.g. CCTV-C01, ACC-D04).
  • Origin & Destination: Specifies exact starting panel and ending camera or reader location.
  • Cable Specification: Lists cable type, length, conductor count, and routing containment.
Standard: Cross-check every pulled cable tag against the project cable schedule table.
(6-7)

Containment & Elevation Drawings

  • Cable Tray Routes: Shows main GI cable tray pathways above false ceilings.
  • Conduit Elevation: Specifies PVC conduit drop positions down building walls.
  • Sizing Specs: Indicates tray width (e.g. 300mm GI tray) and conduit diameter (e.g. 25mm PVC).
Site Standard: Install cable tray containment strictly along approved MEP drawing routes.
(6-8)

Risers & Building Vertical Distribution

  • Vertical Riser Shafts: Shows multi-story backbone cabling running vertically up electrical shafts.
  • Floor Interconnects: Shows fiber optic and Cat6 link connections between floor distributor racks.
  • Firestop Seals: Indicates mandatory intumescent firestop seals at every floor slab penetration.
Safety Code: Seal all floor riser cable penetrations with approved firestop mortar.
(6-9)

As-Built Drawings & Red-Line Markups

  • Red-Line Markups: Hand-mark actual field route changes in red pen on working site drawings.
  • As-Built Finalization: Update CAD drawings to reflect final installed camera and cable positions.
  • Client Handover: As-built drawings are mandatory for project handover sign-off and AMC servicing.
Handover Requirement: Submit final CAD As-Built drawings upon job completion.
(6-10)

Practical Blueprint Reading Lab

  • Task 1: Read a sample SLD diagram and identify MCCB breaker Amp ratings.
  • Task 2: Trace camera locations on a floor plan CAD drawing using the legend.
  • Task 3: Perform a red-line markup update for a modified field camera cable route.
Lab Goal: Complete drawing audit checklist and verify cable schedule tags against CAD plan.
