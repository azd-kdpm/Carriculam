Stage 2: Analog CCTV & Video Systems

(9-2)

Legacy CVBS (Composite Video Blanking Sync)

  • NTSC / PAL Standards: Analog television standards outputting 700 TV Lines (TVL) resolution (~0.4 Megapixels).
  • 1Vp-p 75-Ohm Signal: Composite video signal carries brightness (Luminance Y) and color (Chrominance C) on one wire.
  • Resolution Limits: CVBS cannot exceed 960H resolution due to color subcarrier frequency limits.
Legacy Limit: CVBS max resolution is capped at 700 TVL (~960x576 pixels).
(9-3)

HD-TVI (High Definition Transport Video Interface)

  • Open Format Technology: Developed by Techpoint; widely adopted by Hikvision and global manufacturers.
  • High Resolution over Coax: Transmits uncompressed 1080p, 4MP, and 8MP 4K video over standard RG59 coaxial cables.
  • Long Distance Transmission: Supports up to 500 meters transmission over RG59 coax without signal repeaters.
Compatibility: HD-TVI transmits uncompressed 4K video over legacy RG59 coax cables.
(9-4)

HD-CVI (High Definition Composite Video Interface)

  • Dahua Proprietary Tech: Developed by Dahua Technology; features automatic signal compensation (ASC).
  • 3 Signals on 1 Cable: Transmits Video, Audio, and 2-way PTZ Telemetry Data simultaneously over a single coaxial cable.
  • PoC (Power over Coax): Delivers DC power and 4K video concurrently over the same coaxial conductor.
Key Feature: HD-CVI delivers Video, Audio, Data, and PoC Power over a single coax wire.
(9-5)

AHD (Analog High Definition) & OSD Switching

  • Nextchip Open Architecture: Open-source high-definition analog standard widely used in budget CCTV systems.
  • Backward Compatibility: AHD cameras can be switched down to legacy CVBS mode using an OSD cable joystick.
  • High Immunity: Advanced Y/C signal separation reduces high-frequency color noise.
OSD Switch: AHD cameras feature inline OSD joysticks to switch between AHD, TVI, CVI, and CVBS.
(9-6)

4-in-1 Hybrid DVR Multi-Format Auto-Switching

  • Penta-brid DVRs: Modern DVRs automatically detect and decode TVI, CVI, AHD, CVBS, and IP video streams.
  • BNC Auto-Sense: DVR channel auto-negotiates incoming camera resolution and format frequency.
  • System Migration: Allows gradual upgrade of legacy analog CCTV systems to 4K without replacing existing coaxial cables.
Upgrade Tip: Hybrid Penta-brid DVRs allow upgrading old 700TVL cameras to 4K one by one.
(9-7)

Power over Coax (PoC) Technology

  • Concurrent Power & Video: Transmits 52V DC power from DVR to camera over the RG59 coaxial conductor.
  • PoC Diplex Filter: Separates high-frequency HD video data from DC power inside camera and DVR.
  • Installation Speed: Eliminates separate power supply boxes and twin-core power cables at camera points.
PoC Advantage: Cuts installation labor by 50% by eliminating dedicated camera power supplies.
(9-8)

Signal Attenuation & Frequency Loss

  • High Frequency Loss: Higher video resolutions (4K) use higher signal frequencies, causing faster attenuation.
  • Cable Resistance (dB Loss): Longer coax cable runs cause high-frequency color loss and image softening.
  • Copper Quality Impact: Low-quality CCA coax cables drastically reduce HD-TVI distance from 500m to 100m.
Quality Rule: Use 100% solid copper RG59 cable to prevent high-frequency 4K image attenuation.
(9-9)

Video Line Amplifiers & Equalizer Boosters

  • Active Video Equalizer: Boosts high-frequency video signals for extreme coax runs up to 1000 meters.
  • Gain & Sharpness Tuning: Adjust gain knobs to restore video contrast and sharp edges over long cable runs.
  • Interference Suppression: Filters out high-voltage 50Hz induction hum from neighboring power cables.
Field Solution: Install active video equalizers for coaxial cable runs exceeding 500 meters.
