Fiber Physics: Total Internal Reflection

  • Glass Core (n1) vs Cladding (n2): Light signals bounce continuously inside the glass core by total internal reflection.
  • 125μm Standard Cladding: Both Single-Mode and Multi-Mode fibers feature identical 125-micron outer cladding diameters.
  • Immunity to EMI: Glass fibers are 100% immune to electromagnetic interference, lightning strikes, and high-voltage power lines.
Physics Fact: Total internal reflection allows light signals to travel tens of kilometers without electrical noise.
(30-3)

Single-Mode (OS2) vs Multi-Mode (OM3/OM4) Fibers

  • Single-Mode OS2 (Yellow Jacket): 9μm ultra-thin core; uses 1310nm/1550nm lasers for distances up to 40+ kilometers.
  • Multi-Mode OM3/OM4 (Aqua Jacket): 50μm wider core; uses 850nm VCSEL LEDs for short distances up to 300-500 meters.
  • Modal Dispersion Limit: Multi-Mode light bounces along multiple paths, limiting high-speed distance compared to Single-Mode.
Installation Choice: Use Single-Mode OS2 fiber for campus backbones; use Multi-Mode OM4 for internal data centers.
(30-4)

SFP / SFP+ Media Transceivers & Wavelengths

  • SFP (1 Gbps) vs SFP+ (10 Gbps): Hot-swappable optical transceiver modules plugged into switch SFP slots.
  • Dual LC Fiber Connectors: Separate Tx (Transmit) and Rx (Receive) LC fiber strands.
  • BiDi Single-Strand SFP: Uses Wavelength Division Multiplexing (1310nm Tx / 1550nm Rx) over 1 single fiber strand.
Transceiver Rule: Always pair 1310nm SFP modules with Single-Mode OS2 fiber and LC-LC patch cords.
(30-5)

Fusion Splicing vs Mechanical Splicing

  • Precision Electric Arc Fusion: High-voltage electric arc melts glass core ends together with < 0.02dB loss per splice.
  • Precision Cleaving: High-precision diamond wheel cleaver cuts fiber face at exact 90° angle before splicing.
  • Mechanical Splice High Loss: Index-matching gel joints suffer high loss (0.3dB - 0.7dB) and degrade over time.
Quality Standard: ALWAYS use Fusion Splicing for enterprise CCTV fiber backbones to guarantee < 0.05dB loss.
(30-6)

Fiber Connectors: LC, SC, ST & Polish Types (UPC vs APC)

  • Connector Types: Small-Form LC (Push-Pull), Standard SC (Square Snap), and ST (Bayonet Twist).
  • UPC Blue Polish (Ultra Physical Contact): Flat polished end-face; reflection loss -50dB (Standard Data Networks).
  • APC Green Polish (Angled Physical Contact): 8° angled end-face; reflects backscatter into cladding (-60dB loss) for video/RF.
Connector Rule: NEVER plug Green APC connectors into Blue UPC ports - physical angle mismatch destroys glass faces!
(30-7)

Optical Link Budget Calculations (dB & dBm Math)

  • Transceiver Power Budget: SFP Tx Output (-9 dBm) minus Receiver Sensitivity (-20 dBm) = 11 dB Max Available Margin.
  • Loss Factor Formula: Cable Attenuation (0.35dB/km) + Splice Loss (0.05dB x 4) + Connector Loss (0.3dB x 2).
  • Safety Margin: Always retain at least 3 dB safety headroom margin for fiber aging and future repairs.
Calculation Example: 10km Fiber Run = 3.5dB Cable + 0.2dB Splices + 0.6dB Connectors = 4.3dB Total Loss (Passes 11dB Budget!).
(30-8)

OTDR (Optical Time Domain Reflectometer) Trace Testing

  • Radar Light Reflection: Sends high-speed laser pulses down fiber and measures backscattered light over distance.
  • Fault Location Pinpointing: Locates exact distance (e.g. 2,415 meters) to cable break, sharp bend, or bad splice.
  • OTDR Event Graph: Fusion splices show step-down drops; connectors show sharp reflective peaks.
Diagnostics Standard: Use OTDR testing to produce graphical proof of link distance and splice quality.
(30-9)

Optical Power Meter (OPM) & Visual Fault Locator (VFL)

  • Visual Fault Locator (VFL Pen): 650nm bright red laser pen reveals micro-bends and fiber breaks glowing through jacket.
  • Optical Power Meter (OPM): Measures actual optical signal strength in dBm at 850nm, 1310nm, or 1550nm.
  • Cleanliness Inspection: 85% of fiber failures are caused by microscopic dust on connector end-faces (clean with isopropyl alcohol).
Field Tool: Use VFL red laser pen for fast visual check of fiber patch cords and splice trays.
