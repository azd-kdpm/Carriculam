IP Camera Hardware Processing Pipeline

  • Optical Lens & CMOS Sensor: Captures light photons and converts them into raw digital pixel signals.
  • Image Signal Processor (ISP): Performs exposure adjustment, wide dynamic range (WDR) processing, and noise reduction.
  • SoC Video Compression Encoder: Compresses raw video into H.264/H.265 bitstreams in real time.
  • Gigabit Ethernet Output Port: Packages compressed video frames into IP network packets (RTSP / ONVIF).
Hardware Concept: Unlike analog cameras, IP cameras encode and compress video internally inside the camera body.
(17-3)

RTSP Protocol & Stream URL Structures

  • Real-Time Streaming Protocol: Industry-standard Layer 7 network protocol (Port 554) for controlling media stream delivery.
  • RTSP Syntax Structure: rtsp://[username]:[password]@[IP_Address]:[Port]/[stream_path]
  • Hikvision RTSP Example: rtsp://admin:pass123@192.168.1.100:554/h264/ch1/main/av_stream
  • Dahua RTSP Example: rtsp://admin:pass123@192.168.1.100:554/cam/realmonitor?channel=1&subtype=0
Integration Tip: Use RTSP stream URLs to pull direct live camera feeds into third-party VMS software or VLC media player.
(17-4)

Dual-Stream Encoding (Main Stream vs Sub-Stream)

  • Main Stream (High Resolution): Full 4K/4MP resolution recorded directly onto NVR hard drives for forensic detail.
  • Sub-Stream (Low Resolution): Lower D1/CIF resolution (e.g. 640x360) sent to smartphone apps and multi-screen video walls.
  • Third Stream (Triple Streaming): Optional third stream dedicated to AI video analytics or remote web monitoring.
Bandwidth Optimizer: Use Sub-Stream for 16-channel split screens to prevent PC graphics processor overload.
(17-5)

Unicast vs Multicast Video Streaming

  • Unicast Streaming (One-to-One): Dedicated video stream transmitted to each individual client; 5 users = 5x bandwidth load.
  • Multicast Streaming (One-to-Many): Single stream transmitted to a multicast IP address (224.0.0.0/4); network switches replicate packets to viewers.
  • IGMP Snooping Required: Managed switches require IGMP Snooping enabled to prevent multicast flooding on non-viewing ports.
Enterprise Tip: Enable Multicast on large sites where more than 5 security guards view the same camera stream simultaneously.
(17-6)

Frame Rate Sizing (FPS vs Motion Smoothness)

  • 15 FPS (Surveillance Standard): Provides smooth fluid motion for general building hallways, offices, and parking lots.
  • 25/30 FPS (Full Real-Time): Required for casino gaming tables, license plate capture, and high-speed cash counting.
  • Storage Impact: Dropping frame rate from 30 FPS to 15 FPS reduces storage capacity requirements by almost 45%.
Sizing Rule: 15 FPS is the ideal industry sweet spot balancing smooth motion playback with storage savings.
(17-7)

Resolution Grid & Pixel Density (1080p, 4MP, 4K)

  • 2MP / 1080p (1920x1080): Standard entry-level resolution yielding 2.07 million total pixels per frame.
  • 4MP / 2K (2560x1440): Double the pixel count of 1080p (3.68 million pixels); ideal for wide outdoor areas.
  • 8MP / 4K Ultra HD (3840x2160): 8.29 million total pixels; allows digital zooming into facial features across large plazas.
Pixel Density Rule: Higher resolution allows digital zooming without losing forensic clarity.
(17-8)

Variable Bitrate (VBR) vs Constant Bitrate (CBR)

  • Variable Bitrate (VBR): Dynamic bitrate adjusts automatically to scene activity; saves bandwidth during static nights.
  • Constant Bitrate (CBR): Locks bitrate to a fixed target (e.g. 4Mbps); guarantees predictable storage sizing regardless of motion.
  • VBR Cap (VBR with Max Limit): Prevents bitrate spikes during high-motion events while retaining static scene savings.
CCTV Standard: Use VBR with a Maximum Bitrate Cap for optimal storage efficiency and quality protection.
(17-9)

Edge Storage Failover & ANR (Auto Network Replenishment)

  • On-Camera MicroSD Card: Records video locally on camera internal SD card if network switch or NVR connection fails.
  • Auto Network Replenishment (ANR): Upon network restoration, NVR automatically detects gap and pulls missing video off SD card.
  • Zero Video Loss: Guarantees 100% continuous recording coverage during network outages or switch reboots.
Reliability Standard: Install High-Endurance MicroSD cards in all critical IP cameras for ANR failover protection.
